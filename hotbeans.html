HOT BEANS SOUP (Filipino)

Our halal soup that opens our appetite. Taste Atlas describes sinigang as a “hot Filipino soup consisting of sampalok (fruits of the tamarind tree),” with its “basic broth usually consisting of rice washing, with the addition of a souring agent.” Aside from sampalok, the guava fruit can also be used, as well as calamansi, santol, unripe mango, or kamias. Sinigang is usually made with the following vegetables: radish, tomato, onions, eggplant, green pepper, water spinach (kangkong), long beans (sitaw), oil, water, and salt. Some might choose to add okra, taro (gabi), ginger, miso, or fish sauce. Most Filipinos add protein – pork, beef, chicken, fish, or shrimp – to the savory dish. It is traditionally served as a hot soup with steamed rice.
